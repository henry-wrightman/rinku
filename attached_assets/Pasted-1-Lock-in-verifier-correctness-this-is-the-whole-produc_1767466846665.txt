3) Fuzz the multi-proof encoder/decoder (this is where bugs hide)

Because your packed v5 format has canonical ordering requirements (aux nodes sorted by level then index, etc.) 

WHITEPAPER (10)

, the fastest way to harden is:

Generate random committees (including N not power-of-two) and random signer subsets (including edge cases: k=0, k=1, k≈⅔N, k=N)

Build the “ground truth” tree from the full committee

Produce:

individual proofs (old path)

multi-proofs (new path)

Assert both reconstruct the exact same root tuple (hash + totalWeight)

Then mutate proofs to ensure the verifier rejects:

swap two aux entries (break sorting)

drop one aux entry (missing required)

add an extra aux entry (unused)

duplicate an aux entry

mismatch bitmap popcount vs signer leaves

Your own checklist literally enumerates these rejection rules; fuzzing just turns them into a meat grinder.
