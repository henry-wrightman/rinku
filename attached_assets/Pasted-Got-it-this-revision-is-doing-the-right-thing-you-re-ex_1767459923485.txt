The one “big” correction I’d make next
Profile C verification step 6 is not correct as written

In 2.6 Profile C, you say: “Verify BLS aggregated signature against signer public keys.” 

WHITEPAPER (4)

But with BLS aggregate verification, you typically need either:

aggregate pubkey (sum of signer pubkeys), or

multi-pairing verification across all signer pubkeys (implementation detail), and critically:

a consistent “message” (your signing hash) and proof-of-possession / rogue-key mitigations if you allow arbitrary pubkeys.

Your design partially mitigates rogue keys because pubkeys are committed in the validator set (sum-tree), but you should still state explicitly:

validators register BLS keys with proof-of-possession, or

your chosen BLS scheme/library is PoP-based (or you enforce key validation rules).

Otherwise a crypto reviewer will immediately flag “rogue-key attack on naïve aggregation.”

Minimal paper fix: add one paragraph in Appendix E:

“Validators must register BLS pubkeys with proof-of-possession at staking time; aggregated verification assumes PoP.”
This is standard practice for BLS aggregation.
