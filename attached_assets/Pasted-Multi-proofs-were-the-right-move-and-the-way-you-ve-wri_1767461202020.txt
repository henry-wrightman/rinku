Your auxiliaryNodes: {level,index,node} structure is readable, but it’s not the most size-efficient encoding.

Two ideas to improve it without changing the cryptography:

Per-level bitset + proof-node stream

You already have indices for leaves (signers).

For each level, include a bitset of which nodes are “known,” then stream the missing sibling nodes in canonical order.

This avoids storing (level,index) per node.

Ethereum-style multiproof “flags”

Provide proofNodes[] plus a flags[] bit array that tells the verifier whether to combine from queue vs consume from proof.

Very compact and deterministic.

Either way: you should define one deterministic scheme so two independent implementations don’t disagree on reconstruction.
