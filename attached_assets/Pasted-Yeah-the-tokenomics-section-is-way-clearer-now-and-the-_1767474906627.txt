WHITEPAPER (1)

At 150 RKU/checkpoint, to emit 24,000,000 RKU you need:

checkpoints = 24,000,000 / 150 = 160,000 checkpoints

time = 160,000 × 15s = 2,400,000 seconds ≈ 27.8 days

So in practice, you’d hit the cap in ~1 month, and the whole “18-month halving interval / ~7.5 years emission” story becomes non-operative.

How to fix it (pick one)

Keep 18-month halvings → drastically reduce the per-checkpoint reward.
Rough back-of-napkin: over 5 halving epochs (18 months × 5 = 7.5 years), total emitted is:

R0 * 3,150,000 * (1 + 1/2 + 1/4 + 1/8 + 1/16) = R0 * 6,103,125

To emit ~24,000,000 RKU:
R0 ≈ 24,000,000 / 6,103,125 ≈ 3.93 RKU/checkpoint (instead of 150).
That would make your narrative consistent with your own schedule.

Keep 150 RKU/checkpoint → admit distribution completes in weeks, and redesign the “why” (and probably the validator incentive model post-emission).

Change checkpoint interval (longer than 15s), but that collides with your UX/finality story, so I’d avoid this unless you really want slower checkpoints.
